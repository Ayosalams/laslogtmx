# Customs Broker Authority & Responsibilities

**Domain:** cble_prep | **CFR:** 19 CFR Part 111 | **Updated:** 2026-06-12

## Licensing Scope

A licensed customs broker may transact customs business on behalf of importers and exporters. Customs business includes:

- Preparing and filing entries, declarations, protests, and drawback claims
- Maintaining records required by CBP
- Advising on classification, valuation, and admissibility

Brokers **cannot** substitute for the importer of record's legal responsibility for entry accuracy.

## Power of Attorney (POA)

Before transacting business, a broker must hold a valid CBP Form 5291 (or electronic equivalent via ACE) from the client. POA must specify:

- Principal name and address
- Scope of authority (general or limited)
- Effective dates

## Recordkeeping — 19 CFR § 163

Brokers must retain records for **5 years** from date of entry (or other triggering event per § 163.4). Records include:

- Invoices, packing lists, and correspondence
- Classification and valuation worksheets
- Powers of attorney

## Reasonable Care Standard

Importers must exercise reasonable care. Brokers assist but the importer remains liable for errors. Document your advice in writing when recommending classification or valuation positions.
