# HTS General Rules of Interpretation (GRI) — Basics

**Domain:** cble_prep | **CFR:** 19 CFR Part 177 | **Updated:** 2026-06-15

## GRI 1 — Classification by Heading Terms

Classification starts with the terms of the headings and any relevant Section or Chapter Notes. If goods are described by a heading, classify there unless the Notes say otherwise.

**Example (1430):** A stainless steel kitchen sink imported unassembled is classified by its essential character as a sink (Chapter 73), not as miscellaneous metal parts.

## GRI 2 — Incomplete or Unassembled Articles

- **(a)** Incomplete or unfinished articles with the essential character of the complete article are classified as the complete article.
- **(b)** Mixtures and combinations of materials are classified per GRI 3 if not covered by 2(a).

## GRI 3 — Multiple Headings

When goods could fall under two or more headings:
1. **3(a)** Most specific description prevails.
2. **3(b)** Composite goods / sets — classify by essential character.
3. **3(c)** Last resort — numerically last heading.

## GRI 4 — Most Akin

Goods that cannot be classified by GRI 1–3 are classified under the heading for the most akin goods.

## GRI 5 — Cases and Packaging

- **5(a)** Specially shaped/fitted cases classify with the article if normally sold with it.
- **5(b)** Packing materials and containers classify with the goods unless clearly suitable for repetitive use.

## GRI 6 — Subheading Level

Apply GRIs 1–5 at the subheading level within the same heading.

## CBLE Study Tip

On the exam, always check Chapter Notes before applying GRI 3(b). Notes can exclude or redirect classification entirely — a common trap on classification questions.
