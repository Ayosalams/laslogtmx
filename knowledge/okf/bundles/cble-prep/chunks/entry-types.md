# ACE Entry Types — Quick Reference

**Domain:** cble_prep | **CFR:** 19 CFR Part 141–143 | **Updated:** 2026-06-10

## Consumption Entry (Type 01)

Standard entry for goods imported for use in the U.S. Duties and MPF assessed at entry. Most common entry type for commercial imports.

## Immediate Delivery (Type 11)

Allows release before formal entry summary is filed. Requires entry documents within **10 working days** of arrival (or as extended). Used when speed of release is critical.

## Warehouse Entry (Type 21)

Goods entered into a bonded warehouse. Duties deferred until withdrawal for consumption. Useful for storage, sorting, or re-export strategies.

## Temporary Importation Bond (TIB) — Type 23

Goods imported temporarily without payment of duty if exported within the authorized period (typically 1 year, extendable). Common for trade show exhibits, professional equipment.

## Informal Entry

Simplified process for shipments below the informal entry threshold (value-based; check current CBP limits). Reduced paperwork but same compliance obligations.

## Reconciliation

Post-summary adjustments for issues not known at time of entry (e.g., transfer pricing, related-party valuation). Flagged at entry; must be reconciled within prescribed period.

## Military Time Example

Entry filed **1430** local — consumption entry submitted via ACE before carrier cutoff at **1600** for same-day processing queue.
